For parents and guardians

What this session is, what tools are used, and how volunteer privacy handling stays limited.

This workshop introduces AI in a supervised, youth-appropriate way. It focuses on basic understanding, careful prompting, and checking outputs instead of blindly trusting them.

Independent volunteer support site: this page exists to help families understand one event. It is not an official group or Scouts Canada website.

At a glance

  • Session style: short explanations, guided practice, and a small project.
  • Main tool: ChatGPT or a similar browser-based AI tool, depending on facilitator setup.
  • Safety focus: no sensitive personal data in prompts, adult supervision, and verification habits.

What is ChatGPT in plain language?

ChatGPT is a tool that generates text based on patterns in training data and the prompt it is given. It can be useful for brainstorming, explaining, outlining, and drafting, but it can also be wrong, incomplete, or overly confident.

What youth will practice

  • Writing clearer prompts.
  • Comparing weak and strong outputs.
  • Noticing when an answer needs checking.
  • Using AI as support, not as a replacement for thinking.

Parent / guardian approval path

  • Review the session summary and privacy page.
  • Decide whether you approve participation and tool use.
  • Use the static consent form or the printable checklist path.
  • Keep direct follow-up with volunteers simple and documented.

Volunteer privacy approach

Collect the minimum, store it only for event coordination, restrict access to the organizer and leaders who need it, and delete it after the event follow-up window closes.
