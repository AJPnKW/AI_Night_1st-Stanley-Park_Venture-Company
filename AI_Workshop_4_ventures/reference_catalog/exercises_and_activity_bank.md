# Exercises and Activity Bank

## Purpose

Provide evaluation-ready activity guidance that a facilitator can run directly or adapt into print sheets, slides, or participant web pages.

## Activity 1: First Prompt Win

- Purpose: give every learner an early success within the first 15 to 20 minutes
- Audience fit: complete beginners
- Delivery mode: whole-group guided task
- Time required: 5 to 7 minutes
- Facilitator preparation: prepare one plain prompt and one improved prompt
- Learner steps:
  1. open ChatGPT
  2. run the common starter prompt
  3. inspect the result
  4. improve the prompt once
  5. compare the difference
- Expected outputs: one successful prompt/result pair
- Risks/misuse: learners rushing ahead before the room stabilizes
- Adaptation notes: print the prompt if device friction appears

## Activity 2: Project Pathway Menu

### Pathway A: Event Planner Helper

- Delivery mode: small personal build
- Time required: 10 to 15 minutes
- Learner steps: ask for a checklist, refine with time or budget constraints, prepare a shareable final version

### Pathway B: Scout Activity Idea Builder

- Delivery mode: small personal build
- Time required: 10 to 15 minutes
- Learner steps: generate age-fit activity ideas, test for realism, refine one idea

### Pathway C: Short Script or Message Draft

- Delivery mode: small personal build
- Time required: 10 to 15 minutes
- Learner steps: draft a short announcement or script, tune tone and audience, verify details

### Pathway D: Learn-Something-Quick Research Helper

- Delivery mode: small personal build
- Time required: 10 to 15 minutes
- Learner steps: ask for an explanation, ask follow-up questions, verify one key claim elsewhere

## Activity 3: Stuck Recovery Ladder

- Purpose: reduce freeze and confusion
- Audience fit: any learner who stalls
- Delivery mode: visible prompt ladder
- Time required: 2 to 3 minutes
- Learner steps:
  1. say what you want in one sentence
  2. say who it is for
  3. say what format you want
  4. ask for three options instead of one
  5. ask for a reset prompt from a leader or facilitator
- Adaptation notes: especially useful for neurodivergent learners because the steps are predictable

## Activity 4: Fast Learner Extension

- Purpose: keep quick learners engaged without splitting the workshop
- Delivery mode: optional extension card
- Time required: 5 to 15 minutes
- Extension moves:
  - add tone control
  - add output format control
  - compare two prompt styles
  - convert output into a checklist, table, or script

## Activity 5: Parent Co-Review Task

- Purpose: connect home awareness to workshop readiness
- Delivery mode: pre-work
- Time required: 5 minutes
- Learner steps:
  1. review the participant pre-work page with a parent or guardian
  2. confirm account and device readiness
  3. review the no-personal-data rule

## Evaluation Use

These activities should be scored or reviewed against:

- visible learner success
- clarity of next-step guidance
- low-cognitive-load transitions
- safe-use compliance
